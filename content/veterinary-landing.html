Why Vet Clinics Lose Clients After Hours

Pet emergencies are emotional. When a pet owner's dog is vomiting or their cat isn't breathing right, they're not calmly Googling — they're panicking and calling the first vet they find.

If your clinic doesn't answer, they don't leave a message. They call the emergency vet, pay $400 for a visit, and often switch to that practice permanently because "at least they were there when we needed them."

Even during business hours, your vet techs are in appointments, your front desk is checking in patients, and the phone is competing with the waiting room. Calls slip through.
