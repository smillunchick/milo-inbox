The Emergency Call Problem

HVAC and plumbing is a speed game. When something breaks, homeowners don't comparison shop — they call until someone picks up. The first company to answer gets the job.

Your busiest months are also when you miss the most calls. Your techs are out on jobs. Your office manager is juggling dispatch. And the phone keeps ringing with $300-$2,000 service calls you can't get to.

A traditional answering service helps, but at $300-800/month — with per-call charges on top — it eats into margins fast. And they're reading a script, not actually understanding what's wrong.

What Your AI Receptionist Does

An AI agent trained on your operations that handles service calls like your best dispatcher.

  • 01
    Answers every call instantly — days, nights, weekends
    Peak summer, Christmas Eve, 2am on a Sunday. Milo picks up on the first ring. Homeowners get a professional voice, not a recording.
  • 02
    Triages emergencies vs. routine service
    Burst pipe? Gas smell? No heat with an infant in the house? The AI recognizes true emergencies and escalates immediately to your on-call tech. Routine maintenance request? Books a standard appointment.
  • 03
    Captures job details for your dispatcher
    Address, equipment type, symptom description, urgency level, preferred time. Your dispatcher gets everything they need in a text summary — no callback required to ask basic questions.
  • 04
    Books service appointments into your schedule

  • 05
    Handles pricing and FAQ questions
    "Do you service my area?" "What's a diagnostic fee?" "Do you work on Carrier units?" "Can you come today?" — trained on YOUR service area, brands, and pricing.
  • 06
    Gives your office manager their day back
    Instead of answering 50+ calls per day, your office staff focuses on dispatch, invoicing, and follow-up. Milo handles the phone. They handle the business.
