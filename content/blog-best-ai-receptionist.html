Best AI Receptionist for Small Business (2026)

Updated March 2026 · 8 min read

Every AI answering service promises the same thing: never miss a call again. But they're not all the same — not in pricing, not in quality, and definitely not in what happens to your data when you cancel.

We looked at every major option, talked to small business owners who use them, and ran the numbers. Here's what we found.

The Quick Answer

If you want the best value: build agents you own (that's what we do at Milo). One-time cost, no monthly fees to a vendor.

If you want the easiest setup and don't mind paying $300-1,500/mo indefinitely: Smith.ai is the most polished subscription option.

If you need a free starting point: Google Business Profile messaging handles basic inquiries but isn't a real receptionist.

The 2026 AI Receptionist Landscape

The market has exploded. Two years ago you had maybe 5 options. Now there are 30+. They fall into three categories:

1. Per-Call Subscription Services

You pay monthly based on call volume. The vendor controls everything. Examples: Smith.ai, Ruby, Rosie, Abby Connect.

Pros: Easy setup, no technical knowledge needed.
Cons: Expensive at scale, no ownership, costs grow with your business.

2. DIY Platforms

You get a platform to build your own AI phone system. Examples: Bland.ai, Vapi, Retell.

Pros: Flexible, lower per-minute costs.
Cons: Requires technical skills, time to configure, still a monthly platform fee.

3. Done-For-You Ownership

Someone builds the system for you, then you own it. Example: Milo.

Pros: No technical skills needed, you own everything, lowest long-term cost.
Cons: Higher upfront cost, small ongoing infrastructure fees.

Hidden Costs Nobody Mentions

The switching cost trap: Every month you use a subscription AI receptionist, you're building call flows, training data, and integrations that belong to the vendor. When you leave, you start over. This makes it harder to leave the longer you stay — which is exactly the point.

Per-Call Overages

Smith.ai's starter plan includes 30 calls. A typical dental practice gets 150+ calls/month. Those extra 120 calls at $8 each? That's an extra $960/mo you didn't budget for.

Feature Upsells

Want CRM integration? Extra. Want outbound calls? Extra. Want Spanish language support? Extra. The base price is the starting point, not the final number.

The Opportunity Cost

Every dollar spent renting AI receptionist service is a dollar not spent on marketing, equipment, or hiring. Over 3 years, a $600/mo service costs $21,600. What else could you do with that money?

How to Pick the Right One

Ask yourself three questions:

  1. How long will I need this? If more than 2-3 months, ownership models save money.
  2. How many calls do I get? Per-call pricing kills you at scale. Flat-rate or owned is better for 100+ calls/mo.
  3. What happens when I cancel? If the answer is "everything disappears," you're renting, not buying.
