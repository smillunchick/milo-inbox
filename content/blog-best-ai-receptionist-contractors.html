What Contractors Need (That Dentists Don't)

Most AI receptionist reviews compare features that matter to office-based businesses. Contractors have different requirements:

Emergency call routing

When a homeowner's basement is flooding at 11pm, your AI can't just "take a message." It needs to recognize urgency and call you immediately. Most AI receptionists treat all calls equally. That's dangerous for contractors.

Service area qualification

"Do you serve [city]?" is the most common contractor question. Your AI needs to know your service area boundaries and route or decline accordingly — before you waste 20 minutes driving to a job you can't take.

After-hours handling that actually works

Contractors get 30-50% of their calls outside business hours. An AI that just says "we're closed, call back tomorrow" is losing you the customer. It should qualify the lead, quote next-day availability, and book the appointment on the spot.

Seasonal pricing awareness

"How much for an AC tune-up?" The answer changes by season, by system type, and by whether it's an emergency. Your AI needs enough context to give ballpark ranges without over-promising.

Integration with contractor tools

ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, GorillaDesk — these are what contractors actually use. A receptionist that doesn't connect to your scheduling tool creates more work, not less.

FAQ

Can AI really handle contractor calls? Customers ask complicated stuff.

Modern AI handles 90-95% of contractor calls perfectly: scheduling, service area questions, pricing ballparks, hours, directions, and basic troubleshooting questions. The other 5-10% (complex estimates, warranty disputes, angry customers) get routed to you directly. You're not replacing yourself — you're filtering out the calls that don't need you.

What about emergency calls? I can't have AI telling someone with a burst pipe to "leave a message."

Any AI receptionist worth using has emergency detection and immediate routing. At Milo, we configure urgency keywords (flooding, gas smell, no heat, leak) to trigger an immediate call-through to you or your on-call tech. The AI doesn't take a message — it patches the caller through.

I'm not tech-savvy. Can I still use these?

Yes. Every option on this list handles the technical setup. The self-serve options (CallBird, My AI Front Desk) walk you through forwarding your phone number. The done-for-you options (Milo, SkipCalls, Smith.ai) handle everything — you just provide your business information.

Will my customers know they're talking to AI?

Some will, most won't. But here's the thing contractors consistently tell us: customers would rather talk to a helpful AI that answers immediately than leave a voicemail nobody returns for 4 hours. Speed of response matters more than who's responding.

What about Spanish-speaking customers?

Most AI receptionists support multiple languages. Milo includes Spanish, and 20+ other languages at no extra cost. CallBird locks multilingual support behind their Enterprise tier ($197/mo). Check each provider's language support if this matters for your service area.

I already use ServiceTitan. Will these integrate?

Milo and SkipCalls integrate with ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, and Jobber. CallBird requires their Enterprise plan for CRM integration. My AI Front Desk has limited scheduling integrations. Check the specific tool integration before committing.
