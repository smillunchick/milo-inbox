The Missed Call Cost Formula

Every missed call has a calculable cost. Here's the formula:

Monthly cost of missed calls = (Monthly calls) × (Miss rate) × (% that are potential customers) × (Average job/case value) × (Close rate)

Let's make it concrete. A typical local service business gets 150 calls per month. They miss 62% of them. About 40% of callers are potential new customers. The average job is worth $500. Their close rate on answered calls is 30%.

150 × 0.62 × 0.40 × $500 × 0.30 = $5,580/month in lost revenue

$5,580. Every month. Not hypothetical — that's revenue walking out the door because nobody picked up the phone.

The Compounding Problem

Missed calls don't just cost you one job. They compound in three ways:

1. Lost lifetime value

A new dental patient isn't worth $800 (one cleaning). They're worth $15,000-25,000 over their lifetime. An HVAC customer isn't worth $350 (one repair). They're worth $8,000-12,000 in maintenance contracts, replacements, and referrals. Every missed call costs you the entire relationship, not just one transaction.

2. Negative reviews from unanswered calls

"I called three times and nobody answered" is the second most common 1-star review for service businesses (after "they were late"). Those reviews suppress your Google ranking, which reduces future calls, which reduces future revenue. It's a death spiral.

3. Competitor advantage

When someone calls you and you don't answer, they call your competitor. If your competitor answers, they don't just win that job — they win that customer's loyalty, reviews, and referrals. Your missed call is your competitor's marketing.
