COMP:003 // the real cost

AnswerConnect's hidden cost problem

AnswerConnect advertises starting at $325/month for 200 minutes. Sounds reasonable — until you do the math on actual usage.

The Minute Trap

200 minutes sounds like a lot. But if your average call is 3-4 minutes, that's only 50-65 calls per month. Most service businesses handle 150-300+ calls monthly. At AnswerConnect's overage rates ($1.65-$1.85/minute), those extra calls add up fast.

A typical month for a busy practice: 200 included minutes + 200 overage minutes = $325 base + $350 overage = $675/month.

12-Month Real-World Comparison

AnswerConnect (moderate usage): ~$500/mo x 12 = $6,000

Milo: $399 setup + ($40/mo x 12) = $879

You save $5,121 in year one. That's enough to hire a part-time employee.

The Contract Question

AnswerConnect's pricing improves with longer commitments. But locking into a 12-month contract for an answering service means you're committed to their pricing even if your needs change, a better option emerges, or your call volume drops.

With Milo: no contract. You pay $399, you own the system, and your only decision is whether to keep the $40/month infrastructure running.

COMP:005 // honest take

Who should stick with AnswerConnect

We believe in being honest about fit:

  • High-emotion industries where most calls involve distress (crisis hotlines, grief counseling) — human empathy matters more than efficiency.
  • Businesses that need outbound calling — AnswerConnect offers outbound services; Milo focuses on inbound.
  • Companies that genuinely prefer human-only interactions as a brand value — some luxury services position this as a differentiator.

For everyone else — especially service businesses watching $4,000-$8,000/year flow to an answering service — the AI alternative is overwhelmingly better economics.
