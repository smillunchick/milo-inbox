## 1. Performance-Based Pricing — Model & Recommendation

### The Signal
Reddit and industry forums show clear demand for "pay only if it works" AI pricing. Dave Friedman (Substack) argues SaaS pricing won't work for AI agents because output is dynamic. Revenue Share Digital requires established operators and charges per lead or % of revenue.

### Pricing Models That Work

| Model | Structure | Risk Profile | Best For |
|-------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Pure PPL** | $0 setup + $X per qualified lead | High risk — cash flow unpredictable | Trust-building with skeptical prospects |
| **Pure Revenue Share** | $0 setup + X% of revenue generated | Highest risk — dependent on client conversion | High-ACV service businesses |
| **Hybrid** | Small retainer ($199-499/mo) + per-outcome bonus | Moderate risk — stable base + upside | **Recommended for Milo** |
| **PPL → Retainer** | Start PPL, transition after proof | Front-loaded risk, back-loaded stability | Relationship-building strategy |

### Recommended Milo Approach: "Prove It" Tier

**Structure:** $0 setup + $99/qualified lead (capped at $999/mo) for 90 days, then convert to one-time purchase or managed plan.

**Why this works for Milo:**
- Eliminates the #1 objection ("how do I know this works?")
- $99/lead is well below B2B average ($150-$500 for qualified appointments)
- Cap at $999/mo protects us from margin erosion
- 90-day window creates natural conversion point
- If the leads are good, the client WANTS to switch to owned ($399-$2,499) to remove the per-lead cost

**Risks:**
- Cash flow: ~$0-999/mo per client vs. $399-2,499 upfront
- Attribution: Need clear definition of "qualified lead" (booked appointment? answered call? lead captured?)
- Adverse selection: Clients with worst lead flow are most attracted to PPL
- Scope creep: "The leads weren't qualified enough" disputes

**Mitigation:**
- Require clients to have minimum existing call volume (10+ calls/week)
- Define "qualified" upfront in writing (e.g., caller booked an appointment OR left contact info for callback)
- Track everything in a shared dashboard
- Cap at 5 "Prove It" clients simultaneously while testing

### Unit Economics

| Scenario | Monthly Revenue | Margin (est.) |
|----------|----------------|---------------|
| Client gets 5 qualified leads/mo | $495 | ~85% |
| Client gets 10 qualified leads/mo | $990 (near cap) | ~90% |
| Client converts to owned after 90 days | $399-2,499 one-time | ~80% |

**Breakeven:** If 3/5 "Prove It" clients convert to owned within 90 days, total revenue = ~$3K-$8K vs. ~$2K-5K from straight one-time sales. Plus the 2 remaining PPL clients generate ongoing revenue.
